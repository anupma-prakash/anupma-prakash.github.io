The Earth is a dynamic system that undergoes spatial and temporal changes in response to natural and human induced forces. In the Polar Regions such changes are amplified, making these regions particularly suitable to study and understand processes operating in the Earth system. The Polar Regions, by virtue of their location, are remote and often inaccessible. Field based investigations of the entire Polar Region are very difficult, if not impossible. Satellite remote sensing provides a low cost solution to obtain excellent coverage of the Polar Regions from a vantage point in space.
