Research interest

My research interest lies in using remote sensing and geographic information system (GIS) techniques for Mapping Earth Surface Composition and Change. Changes that are visible on the surface are a result of complex processes operating in the Earth system. These processes hold clues, not only to the current state of the Earth's surface, but also to its past evolution, and its future manifestation.

Examining these cause-effect process requires an interdisciplinary approach. When the Earth system behaves as predicted it is encouraging; when it does not it is fascinating. The interdisciplinary approach helps to understand the interconnections, and appreciate the uncertainties in the Earth system. The different tabs on my research website will provide you a glimpse and pointers to some fascinating interdisciplinary research areas I have been involved with.
