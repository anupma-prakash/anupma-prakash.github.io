Estimating the depth of buried hot features from thermal IR remote sensing data: a conceptual approach

A. Prakash, R.G.S.Sastry, R.P. Gupta and A.K.Saraf
Department of Earth Sciences, University of Roorkee, Roorkee - 247 667, India


published in
International Journal of Remote Sensing, 16(13), 2503-2510.
Copyright © 1995 by Taylor & Francis Ltd.

Abstract

Estimating the depth of buried thermal features from remotely sensed data is a difficult problem. The theoretical basis discussed in this letter shows that if the time of initiation of heat source is known, then utilising two sets of thermal IR data of different time intances for the same anomaly area, the depth of the buried heat source can be computed. However, if the thermal IR data is collected at time intervals close to the time of initiation of heat source, the knowledge of initial conditions of heat source is obviated. In this latter case, a double ratioing technique, utilizing three sets of thermal IR remote sensing data, acquired at different times for the same area can be used for estimating the depth of buried heat source. Respective sample sets of characteristic curves designed for depth estimation are presented along with results of sensitivity analysis tests performed on simulated data.
