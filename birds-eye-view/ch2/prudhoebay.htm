This is a satellite image of Prudhoe Bay and the surrounding area. On the image you can see small scattered rectangular structures in bright cream and black colors. These are oil pumping stations with gravel pads and drilling platforms. The bright lines with angular bends are roads connecting the stations. Around the Prudhoe Bay area are many black patches, which are lakes. Some of the lakes are partially frozen appearing as blue spots.
   It is interesting to note that these lakes change their boundry and shape with time. Two neighboring lakes may grow and merge. Lakes close to the ocean may also have their sea edge eroded away forming a new bay.
